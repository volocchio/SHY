Common Myths

Don't let misconceptions hold you back from your practice

We hear these all the time — and we love busting them. Here's the truth about hot yoga at SHY.

← Back to New to Yoga

01

"I've never done yoga — I can't come to a group class until I know what I'm doing."

Not true! This is probably the most common thing we hear, and it makes us smile every time. The very best time to come to a yoga class is when you've never done it before.

All of our classes are designed for all levels — from total beginners to seasoned practitioners. Our instructors teach to the room, offering modifications and guidance throughout every class so that no one is ever lost or left behind.

The truth is, beginners often progress the fastest because they come in with open minds and no bad habits to unlearn. You don't need to know anything before you walk through our door — that's what we're here for.

Arrive 10–15 minutes early for your first class, introduce yourself to the instructor, and let us take it from there. We promise — you've got this.

02

"Guys don't do yoga."

Absolutely they do! Some of our most dedicated and advanced practitioners are men. Yoga is one of the best things a man can do for his body and mind, and athletes in particular find it transformative.

The physical benefits are undeniable: improved flexibility, increased strength, better balance, faster recovery from workouts and injuries, reduced lower back pain, and improved joint health.

Mentally, yoga offers stress relief and mental clarity that are harder to find in traditional gym settings. Many men find that a consistent yoga practice helps with focus, sleep, and overall well-being in ways they didn't expect.

From weekend warriors to serious athletes to retirees — men of all ages and fitness levels practice at SHY. Come check it out. We think you'll be surprised.

03

"I'll die in the heat."

You won't! We hear this one a lot, and we completely understand the hesitation. The idea of exercising in a heated room can sound intimidating — but the reality is very different from what most people imagine.

The heat actually helps your muscles warm up more quickly and safely, allowing for a deeper range of motion with less risk of injury. Most students discover that the heat feels wonderful once they're moving.

Important notes: Not all of our classes are hot. We offer classes at varying levels of heat, including warm and room-temperature options. And even in our hotter classes, you are always in control. Rest when you need to. Sit down. Take a child's pose. Listen to your body.

Come well hydrated, don't eat a big meal beforehand, and give yourself grace in your first few classes as your body adjusts. Most people are surprised by how quickly they adapt and how great they feel afterward.

04

"I can't do yoga because of my condition or illness."

Yoga is for every body. In fact, many people come to us specifically because of a health condition, injury, or chronic pain — and yoga has been a significant part of their healing and quality of life.

The original Hot 26 series (also known as Bikram yoga) was specifically designed as a therapeutic practice and has a long track record of helping people with back pain, arthritis, diabetes, thyroid conditions, and many other issues.

We are not doctors and we don't make medical claims. But we do see the changes in our students every day. People who came in with canes and left without them. People who regained mobility they thought was gone forever.

Always consult your doctor before starting any new exercise program. And when you come in, please let the instructor know about any conditions or concerns you're working with so we can best guide and support you. We are here to help you find your practice — whatever that looks like for your body today.

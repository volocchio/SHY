New to Yoga

You are the perfect candidate for the benefits of yoga

Anyone Can Do It

Yoga is not just for the flexible — just the opposite. If you are not flexible, if you cannot touch your toes, or perhaps cannot even see your toes, you are a perfect candidate for the benefits of yoga.

The classes are a series of postures and breathing exercises practiced in a heated room (some classes are hot, some are warm). Most classes are beginner classes — whether you are an experienced yogi or have never exercised in your life, everyone will receive the benefits.

There is a light atmosphere and no pressure to perform. It's up to you to proceed at your own pace and to do as much or as little as you desire. No one will be watching or judging.

The most difficult part of your yoga experience will hands down be getting yourself to the studio for your first class and walking through the door. Let us take care of the rest.

How to Prepare

  • Doors open 30 minutes prior to class start
  • Arrive at least 10–15 minutes early for your first class
  • Come well hydrated
  • No big meals 2–3 hours before class
  • Wear light, breathable clothing (baggy or long clothing may hinder your practice)
  • Bring a bath towel (for hot classes), yoga mat, & water (all available for rent or purchase at the studio)
  • Be prepared to change your life!

It's very difficult to get an idea of what the yoga is about in one class. Come back as soon as you can after your first class. Talk to us, ask us questions. The more you practice, the more you will feel and discover about yourself.

Common Myths

Don't let these misconceptions hold you back.

Myth #1

"I've never done yoga, I can't come to a group class until I know what I'm doing."

Not true! All of our classes are designed for all levels. You'll learn as you go, and our instructors will guide you every step of the way.

Myth #2

"Guys don't do yoga."

Absolutely they do! Many of our regular practitioners are men who come for the strength, flexibility, and stress relief benefits.

Myth #3

"I'll die in the heat."

You won't! The heat helps your muscles warm up safely and allows deeper stretching. Stay hydrated, listen to your body, and take breaks when needed. Not all our classes are hot.

Myth #4

"I can't do yoga because of my condition or illness."

Yoga is for every body. Many people come to us specifically for injury recovery, chronic pain, and health conditions. Talk to us about your needs — we'll help you find the right class.
